History of Islam

Islam is one of the world’s major monotheistic religions, with over a billion followers known as Muslims. It emerged in the Arabian Peninsula in the 7th century AD and is based on the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad, who is considered the last and final messenger of God in a line of prophets that includes figures like Adam, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus. To understand the history and origin of Islam, World History Edu explores the socio-political and religious context of Arabia before Islam, the life of Prophet Muhammad, the revelation of the Quran, and the early expansion of Islam. !

"The Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ" Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in the city of Makkah around 570 AD, into the respected Quraysh tribe. He was known for his honesty and was called "Al-Amin" (the trustworthy) by his people. At the age of 40, he began receiving divine revelations through the Angel Jibreel (Gabriel), which were later compiled into the Quran. His message emphasized the oneness of God (Tawheed), social justice, compassion, and moral conduct.
